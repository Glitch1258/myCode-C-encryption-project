During the Second World War, Turing worked for the Government
Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park, Britain's codebreaking
centre that produced Ultra intelligence. For a time he led Hut 8,
the section that was responsible for German naval cryptanalysis.
Here, he devised a number of techniques for speeding the breaking
of German ciphers, including improvements to the pre-war Polish
bomba method, an electromechanical machine that could find settings
for the Enigma machine. Turing played a crucial role in cracking
